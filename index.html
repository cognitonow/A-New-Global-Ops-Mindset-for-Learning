A New Global Ops Mindset for Learning

The monolithic, authoritarian L&D model is broken. We are moving to a distributed, integrated service where knowledge is created instantly from localised knowledge centers at the speed of need, and embedded directly into the tools our colleagues use every day, empowering them to drive business outcomes.

1. The Challenge & Our Vision

The Monolithic, Authoritarian Model is Broken

The Challenge

The traditional L&D model is too slow and encumbered for today's fast-paced environment. Reliance on a siloed LMS and lengthy eLearning development is no longer viable—the time, cost, and resources required are simply too high for the business outcomes we need to achieve.

Our Vision: A Distributed, Integrated Service

We are moving away from a monolithic, authoritarian model of learning. Our vision is to deliver an invisible, integrated service where knowledge and performance support are distributed throughout the organization, embedded directly into the tools our colleagues use every day.

Our Vision in Action

Our Core Principle:

We want to create a learning culture and opportunities where after the eLearning or workshop, our colleagues continue to discuss, talk, create, and be inspired to do more learning about the topic.

2. The Tech Stack & The New Mindset

From Centralized Creation to a Distributed, Employee-Powered Model

Our Approach

We will move away from a centralized L&D content model and empower our functions to create their own knowledge ecosystems.

The primary input for these ecosystems will be our vibrant knowledge communities, ensuring content is relevant, timely, and created by those who know it best.

3. The Content Strategy

From "We Can't" to "Imagine If!"

Fostering a "What If?" Culture

Our goal is to change the mindset from "we can't" to "imagine if!" These are directly achievable outcomes. During my "AI at Primark" campaign, the best responses started with "What if?" Maisie asked, "What if we could have an AI Hiring Manager to test out our new model?" This is the innovation we will unlock.

Knowledge Influencer

Knowledge Creation Events

Communities of Practice

Shifting to a Social Model

In the traditional monolithic model, "engagement" was a desired outcome we had to push for. We are moving to a distributed model where motivation is driven by intrinsic need and interest.

The engagement model is now completely social. Colleagues participate because:

  • →"I want to do better in my job."
  • →"I want to be part of the AI movement."
  • →"I want to be better."
  • →"I have something to offer."

Potential Additions

Conversational AI is the start. It will be the foundation for better, AI-powered solutions. We want better add-on solutions, and our Knowledge Influencer COPs (Communities of Practice) will have the mandate and autonomy to explore them. I asked the "What if?" question in ROA, and I know what a great experience should be. Our tech stack will empower our people to ask these questions and build the answers.

  • Automated Knowledge Capture

    Imagine the Recruitment team meets monthly to discuss results. This session is recorded and automatically transformed into reusable formats: scripts, presentations, and summaries, ready for immediate use.

  • The Rise of the Functional Wiki

    This knowledge is instantly stored in the Recruitment "Wiki," complete with a change log. This creates an ever-growing knowledge base that is organically grown and collects information in real-time. L&D's role is to provide the tools and framework, not to be the bottleneck.

  • The Future of Onboarding

    Imagine what onboarding looks like with such a rich foundation to draw from. Apart from learning about the company and meeting team members, new hires are also introduced to the key Knowledge Influencers within their function. This sets up immediate, clear paths for development that employees know how to follow from day one.

4. Governance, Scaling & Measurement

Global Standards, Local Empowerment, Business Impact

Governance & Scalability

  • Mandate, Authority, and Goals are Set

    This is a formal business decision. This provides the clear direction and empowerment our people want and justifies the budget for the tools and rewards to make it happen. MYP is a global company goal. Knowledge creation and learning is one way to contribute to a KPI. During MYP Season Leaders are inspired to be give talks, podcast, AMAs and post about MYP all of which is learning and knowledge creation.

  • "Freedom within a Framework"

    Global Ops will own the standards, technology, and core frameworks. Local teams will be empowered to create and adapt content for their specific needs using our centrally-supported tools.

  • Community-Led Quality Control

    The best, most useful content will be upvoted and shared by the community, creating a self-regulating system.

Measurement & Success

Our approach to measurement and success has been fundamentally upgraded. Analytics are now powered by a fully integrated Power BI platform, providing real-time information drawn directly from core company systems. This interconnected ecosystem breaks down previous data silos, creating a seamless flow of information from learning engagement (LMS) to employee feedback (Pulse) and ultimately to business KPIs. This strategic shift allows us to transition from reactive analysis of past performance to proactive, data-driven decision-making.
